Why Healthcare Investment Is a Community Decision

When we invest in medical ventures, we're not chasing returns — we're building the infrastructure of care. Every Health-Tech fund we back, every clinical space we help create, is a statement: that capital can serve people before profit. At DKF, we believe the strongest investment thesis is one rooted in community — because healthier communities build stronger economies, and stronger economies fund better healthcare. It's a cycle we're proud to fuel.

DKF at the Swiss Friends of Sheba Gala — 20 Years of Healing & Hope

April 2026

The David Kipnis Foundation joined 170 distinguished guests at the Carlton Hotel St. Moritz for the 20th anniversary Healing & Hope Gala, hosted by the Swiss Friends of Sheba Medical Center. The evening celebrated two decades of support for Sheba — the Middle East's largest medical center — with a cumulative €16.5 million raised over 20 years. DKF founders Alin Kipnis, Ira Fradkin, and Karine Bar Or attended the event, reinforcing DKF's commitment to advancing healthcare through strategic philanthropy. The gala was dedicated to the Ellen Ringier Rehabilitation Wing at Sheba's Children's Hospital, a cause closely aligned with DKF's mission of building clinical spaces and supporting medical innovation.
